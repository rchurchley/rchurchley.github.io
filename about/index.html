I’m a software developer, mathematician, crafter, photographer, and generally curious person. I tend to get excited about oddly specific things. I live in Vancouver with my spouse Leah and our two cats, and currently work at SAP. My full name is globablly unique, so it’s pretty easy to find what I’m up to online (though I tend to be pretty quiet).

I dabble in lots of different hobbies, including photography, biking, origami, and cross-stitch. In the past, I’ve contributed to science outreach programs, played cricket, and played in taiko drumming groups. I spend much of my spare time going for walks, listening to podcasts, and discovering mildly interesting facts.

About this site

This website is a living archive of my personal projects and other creative endeavours. Posts in each section are generally listed in reverse chronological order according to the time of first publication, but I often revise and add to old pages when I am inspired to revisit their ideas.

Although I started publishing at this domain name in 2008, some content dates as far back as 2003, having previously appeared on my now-defunct earlier websites: the non-sequitur photoblog Gee, that’s random, my high school journal Wandering Thoughts, and the photo collage webcomic Isotopes of Bismuth.
